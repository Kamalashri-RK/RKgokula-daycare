Our Curriculum

A balanced approach that nurtures children's curiosity, values, creativity and overall development.

Our Learning Approach

At Radha Krishna Gokula, learning happens through play, exploration and meaningful experiences. Our curriculum supports children's intellectual, emotional and social development while encouraging curiosity, creativity and joyful discovery.

Children learn best when they feel safe, supported and inspired. Our environment encourages children to ask questions, explore ideas and develop confidence through everyday learning experiences.

Areas of Development

Language Skills

Stories, rhymes and conversations that develop vocabulary and communication.

Cognitive Skills

Puzzles, sorting games and activities that develop thinking and problem solving.

Social Skills

Group activities that encourage sharing, cooperation and friendship.

Physical Development

Outdoor play and movement activities that build coordination and strength.

Value & Character Learning

At Radha Krishna Gokula, children are gently introduced to values such as kindness, respect, gratitude and responsibility through stories, conversations and daily routines.

Simple teachings inspired by the wisdom of the Bhagavad Gita help children understand honesty, discipline and compassion in a practical and age-appropriate way.

Learning by Age

1 – 2 Years

  • Sensory exploration
  • Music and rhymes
  • Safe play activities
  • Motor skill development

2 – 3 Years

  • Language development
  • Creative play
  • Simple storytelling
  • Social interaction

3 – 5 Years

  • Early literacy and numbers
  • Creative arts and crafts
  • Yoga and meditation
  • Problem solving activities

Yoga, Meditation & Value Wisdom

At Radha Krishna Gokula, practices such as yoga, meditation and value-based stories inspired by the Bhagavad Gita are part of the daily routine rather than occasional activities.

Just as children benefit from regular meals and playtime, we believe that calm reflection, mindful breathing and gentle movement should be practiced every day. These activities help children develop focus, self-awareness, emotional balance and positive habits from an early age.

Through simple and child-friendly sessions, children gradually learn discipline, patience, gratitude and respect for themselves and others. Our goal is to make these practices a natural and joyful part of their daily life.
